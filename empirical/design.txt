Section: Action Space Design Has Not Reached Consensus 
[assets/consensus.png]
Bullet points:
Action space specification is a critical design choice in imitation-based robotic manipulation policy learning. 
The chosen action space fundamentally shapes the optimization landscape and learning dynamics of robotic policies. 
Action spaces are often selected using ad-hoc heuristics or legacy conventions, leading to an unclear understanding.
We conduct a large-scale, systematic empirical study to investigate the role of action space design. 
Our results reveal that action space choices have significant and nuanced effects on policy learning performance.

Section: Overall Takeaways
[assets/main_table.png] (takes up a whole line)
[assets/radar.png] left, [assets/advanced.png] right
(1) The superiority of delta actions remains consistent across diverse learning regimes. 
(2) Joint-space actions benefit exceptionally from stronger modeling capacity and extensive training.
(3) Task-space representations demonstrate a pronounced advantage in generalized settings such as cross embodiment and transfer learning regimes.


Section: Scaling Experiments
(1) Delta actions serve as a superior temporal abstraction for modern policy backbones.

We partition the workspace into a `6x6` grid and evaluate each policy across the resulting spatial bins. For each action representation, we first compute the success rate within each grid cell, and then summarize the degree of spatial heterogeneity by taking the standard deviation of these per-cell success rates across all locations.
